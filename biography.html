Full biography

Suvan Agarwal is a 22 year old American classical guitarist, whose playing has been described as "absolute mastery: brilliant technical command, gorgeous tone and sophisticated pacing and nuance" (Stephen Aron). He has won prizes at the Louisville Collegiate Guitar Competition, Guitar Foundation of America International Youth Ensemble Competition, and Tuesday Musical Scholarship competition, where in addition to first prize in the guitar category, he won the audience prize in the final round open to winners from all instrument categories. He has performed in both the United States and Japan.

Suvan started playing guitar at the age of five taught by Frank Longay, creator of the Suzuki guitar method. After Frank's untimely passing in 2011, he took a break from guitar due to lack of interest, eventually returning to lessons in high school with a renewed love of music. Under the guidance of Alexandra Viloteau (née Iranfar), he rapidly improved, and during his senior year he made the decision to enter Oberlin College and Conservatory's double degree program to study physics and guitar performance. Suvan now studies with Stephen Aron and is due to graduate with degrees in guitar performance and physics in Spring 2024. He will begin pursuing a masters degree at the Royal Conservatoire of Scotland starting in Fall 2024 after receiving a full ride scholarship from the Associated Board of the Royal Schools of Music (ABRSM). He will study under Matthew McAllister and Allan Neave.

Outside of guitar, Suvan was also a recipient of a National Science Foundation scholarship to conduct geophysics research at the University of Hawaii in 2022, as well as a scholarship from the Friends of the University of Tokyo (FUTI) foundation to conduct geophysics research at the UTokyo Earthquake Research Institute (東京大学地震研究所) in 2023. He studied abroad at Waseda University（早稲田大学）in 2022, and was the first Oberlin College student ever to receive the Bridging Scholarship from the United States-Japan Bridging Foundation. Suvan was born in Japan and speaks both English and Japanese. He is part Indian and part Japanese.
